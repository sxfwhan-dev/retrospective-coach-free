# Example: Product Launch Failure Retrospective

## User Input

```text
I want to review a failed product launch.
The goal was to get 1,000 registered users in the first week, but we only got 260.
We started preparing launch materials two weeks before launch. On launch day, we found that the landing page conversion was low and community promotion performed weakly.
I want to understand the main issues and what to improve next time.
```

## Example Retrospective Coach Output

### Retrospective Conclusion

The core issue was not simply insufficient promotion. The launch exposed that the team had not validated the target audience, message, landing page, and channel response early enough before launch day.

### Facts and Gaps

- Original goal: 1,000 registered users in the first week.
- Actual result: 260 registered users.
- Key gap: registrations reached only 26% of the target.
- Known process: launch materials started two weeks before launch; landing page conversion was found to be low on launch day; community promotion performed weakly.

### Key Findings

1. Launch preparation likely started too late, leaving little validation time.
   - Evidence: launch materials started only two weeks before launch.
   - Confidence: medium.

2. The conversion path was not sufficiently validated before launch.
   - Evidence: landing page conversion was discovered to be low on launch day.
   - Confidence: high.

3. Weak community response may indicate a mismatch in audience, message, or channel fit.
   - Evidence: community promotion performed weakly.
   - Confidence: medium. Community type, reach, and click data are still missing.

### Lessons

- A launch does not begin on launch day. Audience, message, landing page, and channel response should be tested before the main push.
- A registration target should be decomposed into funnel metrics such as reach, clicks, visits, conversion, and registration.
- Without a small traffic test before launch, launch-day problems become much harder to control.

### Improvement Actions

1. Run a small conversion-path test 14 days before the next launch.
   - First step: send small traffic from one target channel and check reach, clicks, and registrations.
   - Check signal: landing-page click rate and registration conversion rate.
   - Readiness: baseline needed first.

2. Test channel response 7 days before launch.
   - First step: test 2-3 message angles in target communities.
   - Check signal: engagement rate, click rate, and useful feedback count.
   - Readiness: baseline needed first.

3. Break the 1,000-user target into funnel metrics.
   - First step: define target values for reach, clicks, visits, and registrations.
   - Check signal: deviation at each funnel layer.
   - Readiness: ready to start.

### Missing Information

- Landing page visits and registration conversion rate.
- Community reach, clicks, and interaction data.
- Product positioning, target user profile, and launch channel list.

### Next Review Focus

Next time, focus on whether pre-launch validation was early, small, and measurable enough.
