# Example: Personal Growth Retrospective

## User Input

```text
I want to review my learning this month.
My original goal was to study English for 5 hours every week, but I only completed about 6 hours in total.
The first two weeks were okay, but once work became busy in the last two weeks, I stopped.
I want to know how to make next month more stable.
```

## Example Retrospective Coach Output

### One-Sentence Conclusion

The goal was not missed because of a lack of motivation alone. The bigger issue was that the learning plan depended on spare time and was not tied to fixed triggers, work-pressure buffers, or a minimum viable version.

### 3 Key Judgments

1. The goal was not broken down in a stable enough way.
   - Evidence: the goal was 5 hours per week, but the plan broke when work became busy.
   - Confidence: high.

2. The learning action did not have a low-friction backup version.
   - Evidence: there was no minimum learning action maintained during busy weeks.
   - Confidence: medium.

3. The learning rhythm did not absorb work fluctuations.
   - Evidence: the first two weeks were okay, but the last two weeks stopped because of work pressure.
   - Confidence: high.

### 3 Lessons

1. Personal growth goals need both an ideal version and a minimum version for busy periods.
2. Weekly goals should be attached to fixed time slots, otherwise urgent work can easily displace them.
3. Maintaining continuity matters more at first than maximizing each session's length.

### 3 Next Actions

1. Define a minimum version for next month.
   - Action: complete either 10 minutes of listening or 10 vocabulary items per day to maintain continuity.
   - Check signal: number of non-break days per week.
   - Readiness: ready to start.

2. Lock two non-negotiable learning blocks.
   - Action: choose two 30-minute slots each week, such as Tuesday and Thursday evenings.
   - Check signal: number of fixed learning blocks completed.
   - Readiness: ready to start.

3. Do a 10-minute weekly mini-review every Sunday.
   - Action: record total learning time, interruption causes, and next week's risks.
   - Check signal: whether the weekly review was completed.
   - Readiness: ready to start.

### Missing Information

- Exact dates and durations of this month's study sessions.
- The busiest work periods.
- Whether the English goal is for exams, speaking, reading, or work use.
